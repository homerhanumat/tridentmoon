Postures of the Primary Series

Below are notes I took on one of Andrew Eppler’s training videos. I have supplemented his statement of the vinyāsa count with accurate Sanskrit names of the postures.

sūryanamakāra (Salutations to the Sun)

Andrew says that sūryanamakāra are the meter and scale of the “song” that is the Primary Series.

Standing Sequence

pādāṅguṣṭhāsana (Thumb to Foot Posture)

4 to enter:

  • inhale step left hands to hips look up 1
  • exhale fold forward 2
  • inhale straighten look up 3
  • exhale fold 4

(andrew stays down, Joey does 4 to exit, ending in sama)

pādahastāsana (Hand [under] Foot Posture)

4 to enter:

  • inhale look up hands to hips
  • exhale hands under feet
  • inhale look up
  • exhale fold

4 to exit (ends in sama):

  • inhale look up hands to hips
  • exahle fold
  • inhale up
  • exhale step back to same

trikoṇāsana (Triangle Posture) and parivṛttatrikoṇāsana (Revolved Triangle Posture)

2 e trik 2 change sides 2 e pari tri. (could add 2 as prep as “extra”) 2 exit

parśvakoṇāsna (Side Angle Posture)

2’s for parsva come back to sama after

parivrttaparśvakoṇāsana (Revolved Side Angle Posture)

2 to enter (with 4 “extra” option as prep)

prasāritapādottānāsana (Stretching Out to Spread Feet Posture)

four vinyāsa to enter A

transition to B:

  • inhale stand, arms wide 1
  • exhale hands to hips 2
  • inhale look up 3
  • exhale down 4

transition to C:

  • inhale up hands still on hips1
  • exhale clasp 2
  • inhale look up 3
  • exhale down 4

transition to D:

  • inhale up arms wide 1
  • exhale hands to hips 2
  • inhale look up 3
  • exhale down 4

exit is inhale up arms wide, exhale step forward to sama

parśvottānāsana (Stretching Out to the Side Posture)

inhale step left arms wide exhale hands to reverse namaste inhale turn foward look up ex fold into posture

inhale out ex rotate inhale up ex fold

inhale up and open arms ex trun and step to sama

utthitahastapādāṅguṣṭhāsana (Big Toe of the Foot [grasped] with Risen Hand Posture)

to A:

  • in take toe
  • ex extend

to B:

  • in out to right
  • ex turn head left

to C:

  • ex center
  • in head up and grab
  • ex head down

to D:

  • in head back up
  • ex hands on hips

2-2-3-2

same on the other side

ardhabaddhapadmottānāsana (Stretching out in Half Bound Lotus Posture)

4 to enter 2 to exit (only 2!)

utkaṭāsana (Fierce Posture)

Note: In this posture the body resembles a jagged lightening-bolt; some people say this is why it is “fierce”.

2 to enter but exhale is placeholder

exit is 2 (in up, ex to sama)

vīrabhadrāsana (Auspicious Hero Posture)

Note: “Vīrabhadra” is the name of an incarnation of Śiva. Monier-Williams says:

… in the Vāyu-Purāṇa he is said to have been created from Śiva’s mouth in order to spoil the sacrifice of Dakṣa, and is described as having a thousand heads, a thousand eyes, a thousand feet, and wielding a thousand clubs; his appearance is fierce and terrific, he is clothed in a tiger’s skin dripping with blood, and he bears a blazing bow and battle-axe; in another Purāṇa he is described as produced from a drop of Śiva’s sweat.

5 to enter:

  • in step arms wide
  • ex turn lunge
  • in up
  • ex placehold

5 to transition:

  • exhale 1 arms out to side
  • inhale turn
  • exhale turn and lunge
  • in up
  • ex placehold

thne to B: ex down

transition: in turn, ex settle in

exiting:

  • ex down srep back
  • in updog
  • ex down
  • inhale jump through

Seated Postures

daṇḍāsana (Stick Posture)

paścimottānāsana (West Stretching out Posture)

ex, in ex then start 5 breaths

three versions

3-2-2

pūrvottānāsana (East Stretching out Posture)

4 to enter:

  • in head up
  • ex hands back
  • in lift
  • ex

standard jump-back and jump-through: 5 count

  • in release
  • ex back
  • in up-dog
  • ex down-dog
  • in jump through

ardhabaddhapadmapaścimottānāsana (Half Bound Lotus West Stretching out Posture)

  • in half-lotus
  • ex reach around
  • in look up
  • ex fold forward

So that’s actually a 9-count vinyāsa

But there are two inhales in a row!

exit is:

in undo ex jump back * in up * ex down * in jump * ex lotus * in look up * ex fold

tryaṅgamukhaikapādapaścimottānāsana (Three-Limbed Face to One Leg West Stretching out Posture)

  • in release
  • ex back
  • in up
  • ex down
  • in through into posture and look up
  • ex fold

jānuśīrṣāsana (Head to Knee Posture)

  • 8 to enter
  • 10 to transition to next side

or think of it as 8 to get in, 2 to get out

marīcyāsana (Marīci Posture)

This posture is dedicated to the sage Marīci. There are four variations: A, B, C and D.

  • ex bend knee
  • in forward
  • ex complete the wrap around
  • in look up
  • ex fold forward

10 counts on both sides

B: 10 to get in, both sides

C: 5+3 to get in: ex knee up in twist exwarp around and grab

D: 5 + ex lotus, in knee up, ex twist, in arm up, ex wrap around and grab

nāvāsana (Boat Posture)

5 to enter, begin count in exhale,

inhale up, exhale back into posture

bhujapīḍāsana (Pressure on the Shoulders Posture)

in jump around, ex the start counting

on 5th ex, tuck down to B

kūrmāsana (Tortoise Posture)

officially: in jump around, exhale flatten out

suptakūrmāsana (Sleeping Tortoise Posture)

for the “less advanced” version, it’s 2 to enter

garbhapiṇḍāsana (Embryo in the Womb Posture)

ex into padma, in right hand ex left hand. take 5, then roll 9 times

kukkuṭāsana (Rooster Posture)

you come up into it on an inhale

baddhakoṇāsana (Bound Angle Posture)

no problem

upaviṣṭakoṇāsana (“Entered Into” Angle Posture)

Note: upaviṣṭa can also mean “seated” but here it most likely refer to the way that the forward-folding torso “enters into” the angle formed by the legs.

  • ex spread legs out
  • in grab toes and look up
  • ex fold

then in look up ex grab toes and all up

suptakoṇāsana (Sleeping Angle Posture)

no jump-through transition, instead just in get read ex roll back

in roll up, ex come down

and then do another 5 breaths!

suptapadāṅguṣṭhāsana (Sleeping Thumb to Foot Posture)

There are four stages: A, B, C, D.

  • ex down
  • in leg up
  • ex draw shouler down

then B

then enter C

  • on 5th exhale, flip hand over
  • in bring leg around
  • ex head within

another to count to enter D

Consider taking chakrāsana for the transition out.

ubhayapādāṅguṣṭhāsana (Thumbs to Both Feet Posture)

the roll-back comes after!

ūrdhvamukhapaścimottānāsana (Upward Face West Stretching out Posture)

roll up into this, no jump-through

setubandhāsana (Bridge-Binding Posture)

10 to enter

Again consider chakrāsana for the transition out.

ūrdhvadhanurāsana (Upward Bow Posture)

not more than 3 breaths between

do some kind of forward fold after

Closing Sequence

sālambhasarvāṅgāsana (Posture where All Limbs have Suppport)

in down, ex up

hālāsana (Plow Posture)

karṇapīḍāsana (Pressure on the Ears Posture)

one ex in

ūrdhvapadmāsana (Upward Lotus Posture)

4 to enter

piṇḍāsana (Embryo Posture)

2 to enter

matsyāsana (Fish Posture)

2 to enter

uttānapādāsana (Legs Stretching out Posture)

2 to enter

śīrṣāsana (Head[stand] Posture)

25 breaths, then come down holding for 5, maybe even do that three times

interesting he says in down ex up

bālāsana (Child Posture)

baddhapadmāsana (Bound Lotus Posture)

generally folks just sit down into this after previous

padmāsana (Lotus Posture)

minimum 25 in this posture

prāṇāyāma, mudras encouraged here

tolāsana (Lift Posture)

Note: Monier-Williams cites tolana as “lifitng up” in the Rāmāyana.
